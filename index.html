Self Introduction

Hello, I am Andy Diaz Gomez, a new RN graduate living in Miami; I plan to take my NCLEX between May and June 2026. I am looking for a position as a Registered Nurse. I have a great passion for working in the medical, surgical, emergency and ICU departments where collaboration, effective communication and a composed clinical approach are crucial.

One reason I chose nursing is the close and frequent contact with patients; nurses are often the first to notice subtle changes in a patient's condition. This connection enables early intervention, prompt communication and helps prevent emergencies.

My strengths include empathy, the ability to stay calm under pressure and clear communication. I excel at patient education—simplifying information so that patients understand their care plans, which promotes shared decision-making and self-management.

My clinical experiences include Behavioral Health at Evolutions Treatment Center and Medical-Surgical at Select Medical, where I practiced building trust, using therapeutic communication and providing safe, organized care. Respect, equity, integrity and service are my guiding principles, and I infuse these values into patient interactions and teamwork.

Nursing Philosophy

Nursing, in my opinion, is professional care that uses evidence, clinical judgment, and compassion to uphold human dignity and assist people in healing, adapting, or living well with their illness. Being a bilingual student of nursing, I have witnessed that the results are significantly better when patients understand the plan and feel comfortable asking questions.

Nurses often know patients best because they spend sustained time with them during daily shifts. Much of nursing involves continuous observation, analysis, and timely intervention when a patient’s condition changes. The National Academies report notes that nurses frequently serve as the first point of access to care, especially in community settings, which makes early assessment and timely escalation a core responsibility.

My core beliefs are respect, equity, integrity, and service. Respect is about being attentive and tailoring care to a patient's culture and language. Equity involves recognizing barriers—such as cost, stigma, or low health literacy—and finding sustainable solutions. Integrity is about recognizing boundaries and being truthful. I want to be the nurse who detects changes early, communicates effectively, and doesn't hesitate to raise the alarm when necessary.
